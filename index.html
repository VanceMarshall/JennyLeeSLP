Meet Jenny

I'm Jennifer Lee Marshall, and I'm passionate about helping children find their voice and reach their full communication potential.

As a Kentucky and Ohio licensed speech-language pathologist with my ASHA Certificate of Clinical Competence (CCC-SLP), I bring specialized expertise in pediatric communication and feeding disorders. My educational journey began with a degree in Biopsychology from UC Santa Barbara, followed by a Master's degree in Communication Sciences and Disorders from Michigan State University.

I spent over six years at Cincinnati Children's Hospital Medical Center, where I received intensive training in resonance disorders, augmentative and alternative communication (AAC), multidisciplinary care, and pediatric feeding. I had the privilege of working alongside surgeons and multidisciplinary teams in the Craniofacial Anomalies Team (CFAT) and Velopharyngeal Insufficiency (VPI) clinics, caring for children with some of the most complex communication and feeding needs.

Publications & Presentations

  • Published research in the International Journal of Pediatric Otorhinolaryngology on non-cleft causes of velopharyngeal dysfunction
  • Presented national webinars on Velocardiofacial/22Q Syndrome
  • Featured speaker on communication and feeding concerns in complex genetic conditions

As a member of the American Cleft Palate-Craniofacial Association, I stay connected to the latest research and best practices. Now, in my private practice, I'm honored to serve families right here in Central Kentucky, offering the same high-level, specialized care in a more personal, one-on-one setting. Whether your child has a cleft palate, a speech delay, feeding challenges, or complex needs, I'm here to listen, guide, and support your family every step of the way.

Therapy

Individualized, family-centered therapy designed to help your child communicate more effectively and confidently. I specialize in:

  • Speech sound disorders
  • Language delays and disorders
  • Birth-to-three services (KY First Steps provider)
  • Resonance and voice concerns
  • AAC trials and device support
  • Childhood apraxia of speech
  • Feeding challenges

Each session is tailored to your child's strengths, needs, and goals, with ongoing collaboration with your family.

Screening & Evaluations

Comprehensive assessments to identify your child's strengths and areas of need. I offer screening and in-depth evaluations for speech, language, resonance, and feeding concerns.

My specialized training includes perceptual resonance assessment for children with cleft palate, suspected velopharyngeal insufficiency (VPI), or other craniofacial differences—a rare expertise among speech-language pathologists.

You'll receive clear, detailed feedback and a concrete plan to support your child's development.
